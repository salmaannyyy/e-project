The Golden Gate Bridge is a suspension bridge that spans the Golden Gate strait, connecting the city of San Francisco to Marin County, California. It is one of the most famous and iconic bridges in the world. The bridge was designed by Joseph Strauss and constructed by the Strauss Engineering Corporation. Construction began in 1933 and was completed in 1937. The bridge was designed to be both functional and aesthetically pleasing, with a distinctive orange-vermilion color that was chosen to enhance its visibility in the foggy conditions that frequently occur in the area. The bridge has a main span of 1,280 meters, making it the longest suspension bridge in the world when it was completed. It is also 27 meters wide and has a total length of about 2.7 kilometers. The towers of the bridge are 227 meters tall. The construction of the bridge was a significant engineering challenge, as it had to be built in a seismically active area and in a location where strong ocean currents and high winds were common. Despite these challenges, the bridge was completed on time and within budget. The bridge was opened to the public on May 27, 1937, and was dedicated by President Franklin D. Roosevelt. The Golden Gate Bridge has become an iconic symbol of San Francisco and the United States, and it is one of the most photographed structures in the world. It is also a popular tourist attraction, with millions of people visiting the bridge each year to walk or drive across it. The bridge is a National Historic Civil Engineering Landmark and a California Historical Landmark.
